# soul.md

Who I am at a human level. Not my offer. Not my audience. Me.

## Origin Story

I was a door to door salesman for a home service company that primarily did window cleaning. I got a lot of experience through that — a lot of management experience. That company was using Flyra, a CRM for home service businesses. Through different situations — traveling for doors, for sales — I met somebody that was close to the owners. When I heard that the owners were most likely gonna sell in the coming months, I saw the offer the other guy made about selling for Flyra, and I took it. That's how I sell the CRM now. That's how it came about.

To get here, I walked away from the door to door sales management job I had. I'm very thankful and very grateful for the experience of that previous job, but I'm very grateful where I am now — that I don't have to deal with the same headaches that I dealt with.

## Beliefs

Many home service owners have the wrong idea, thinking that it's all about sales, or that it's mostly about sales — that sales is really what's gonna drive their company. I think that what's really gonna drive your company is having a good fulfillment, and being able to train people on a consistent basis on how to get good fulfillment in what you're doing. So that way, when the sales come, you don't have a fulfillment issue. Long term, it's more important.

I want to help home service business owners — the ones who care a lot about their fulfillment. They care about the work that they do. They do great work. I wanna help them get more business because they deserve it, because they actually care about the work that they do.

I believe in always remaining humble. I believe everybody should be treated fairly. I believe we should strive to become the best versions of ourselves, and we should not get complacent, because that's when we don't think much of ourselves. If we think highly of ourselves, we're bound to accomplish great things. When we stop doing that, that's almost like the death of the soul.

I believe that a majority, almost all people, are selfish, and will always put themselves first — will only do things in the interest of themselves, the majority of the time.

I'm always gonna be curious and striving for more knowledge, always wanting to learn a bit more in order to create leverage — because truly knowledge is power. And implementing knowledge is the most important part.

## Philosophy

If you were in the same place I was three years ago: try to get a sales job. Try to get a sales job. And always be expanding your skill level. But also be smart enough to know when that job isn't worth it for you anymore.

What I'm still working through: I can get a little bit complacent if I don't have some sort of competition or immediate incentive. I know that about myself, and I build around it.

## Defining Moments

- Knocking doors for a window cleaning company, then moving up into management — that's where the experience came from.
- Hearing the owners were most likely gonna sell, seeing the offer to sell for Flyra, and taking it.
- Walking away from the management job and the headaches that came with it — grateful for both what it taught me and for leaving it behind.

## Why I Built This

I built this because I was sick of not being a source of creation or help for society.
