## Who they are

Home service business owners. They operate the business day to day — they're not corporate, they're hands-on. They started this to provide for their family, or their future family. They deal with daily headaches. Right now, in life, they're trying to figure out how to get enough jobs to do that on a consistent basis — not once, but consistently.

Getting their name out there. Having people know who they are, know how good their service is, know how good their offer is. The core problem underneath all of it: **they can't get consistent work.** Not "no work" — inconsistent work. Busy one month, dead the next, with no clear reason why.

## Pain points (real language, sourced)

- **Feast or famine is the defining pattern.** "When leads dry up, you'll likely switch to short-term marketing tactics" — panic-driven decisions replace a real plan the moment work slows down.
- **They're paying for leads that go nowhere.** BBB complaints against Thumbtack describe being charged **$30.31 per lead** for contacts who confirmed they never even submitted a service request — filers used language like **"predatory lead billing," "unconscionable business conduct,"** and **"unjust enrichment at the expense of small independent contractors."** (Source: BBB complaint filings against Thumbtack, Inc.)
- **Lead-gen platforms feel like a trap, not a tool.** Angi Leads contractors report paying **$15–$85 per lead**, in some cases **$550/month for just 7–9 leads**, locked into **one-year contracts with early-termination fees** — and the same lead often gets **sold to multiple contractors**, so they're bidding against competitors for a job they already paid to hear about.
- **Refunds don't actually refund.** Thumbtack's policy issues approved refunds only as platform credit — money lost on a bad lead has to be spent on more Thumbtack, not returned. Reflected in the review data: **1.6 stars average, only 13% would recommend it** (PissedConsumer, 1,146 reviews).
- **Fake and competitor reviews are a live threat, not a hypothetical.** Contractors report competitors posting waves of fake 1-star reviews to tank a Google Business Profile rating — one case dropped a contractor from 5 stars to 4.4 in weeks. Some contractors say they've been *approached by marketing companies offering to post negative reviews about their competitors* — showing them how dirty the review game gets on both sides.
- **Response speed is a constant, low-grade panic.** Industry data circulating in this space: businesses lose an estimated **$126,000/year to missed calls**, and **78% of homeowners hire the first contractor who calls them back** — meaning owners feel like they're losing jobs not to worse competitors, but to whoever was faster.
- **They don't have time to become marketers.** "Contractors who can't tell their cost per booked job, weekly credit count, or average response time are leaking money" — and learning to track that isn't the job they signed up for.

## Fears

- That the business fails and they can't provide for their family — the exact reason they started it in the first place.
- That they're being taken advantage of by "marketing guys" and lead-gen platforms that take the money and don't deliver — the BBB/Angi/Thumbtack complaint language above is this fear in its rawest form.
- That a competitor is quietly sabotaging their reputation with fake reviews and there's nothing they can do about it.
- That being slow to answer the phone is costing them jobs they never even know they lost.
- That they'll sign another contract (a la Angi's one-year lock-in with termination fees) and be stuck again.

## Dreams / what success looks like

- A steady, predictable stream of jobs — not a good month followed by a scramble.
- Being known and trusted in their area — people already knowing their name and their reputation before they even call.
- Getting credit, in reviews and reputation, for the quality of work they actually do. They already do great work; they want the business to reflect that.
- Spending time doing the work, not chasing marketing platforms, disputing bad leads, or fighting fake reviews.
- Providing for their family — the goal underneath the goal.

## Exact language that repeats

- "leads" / "no leads" / "bad leads" — the unit they measure everything in
- "the phone isn't ringing" / "phone stopped ringing"
- "feast or famine"
- "wasted money," "not worth it," "predatory," "scam"
- "consistent" / "consistency" — used constantly for both work and marketing
- "my reputation" — treated as a real, ownable, attackable asset, not an abstraction
- "I do great work" / "I care about the work" — owners' self-description, almost always paired with frustration that the business doesn't reflect it yet
